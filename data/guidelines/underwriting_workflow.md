# Underwriting Workflow Guidelines

## Decision Matrix

### Accept Criteria
ALL of the following must be met:

- No high-severity triggers
- All required information provided
- Hazard scores within acceptable ranges
- Property meets construction standards

### Refer Criteria
ANY of the following:
- Eligibility score 0.5-0.7
- Medium-severity triggers present
- Some required information missing
- Hazard scores in moderate range
- Minor construction standard issues

### Decline Criteria
ANY of the following:
- Eligibility score < 0.5
- High-severity triggers present
- Critical information missing
- Hazard scores in high range
- Major construction standard violations

## Information Requirements

### Always Required
- Applicant name and contact information
- Property address
- Property type
- Coverage amount requested
- Construction year

### Conditionally Required
- Square footage (if coverage > $250,000)
- Roof type (if property > 20 years old)
- Foundation type (if in earthquake zone)
- Number of stories (if condo/townhouse)

### Optional but Helpful
- Recent renovations
- Security systems
- Distance to fire department
- Distance to fire hydrant

## Quality Control Checks

### Automated Checks
- Address validation
- Coverage amount reasonableness
- Property type validation
- Required field completeness

### Manual Review Triggers
- Coverage amount > $1,000,000
- Properties in high-risk areas
- Unusual property characteristics
- Inconsistent information provided

### Documentation Requirements
- All decisions must be supported by citations
- Referral reasons must be specific
- Decline reasons must reference specific guidelines
- File notes must be complete and accurate

## Communication Standards

### Accept Communications
- Clear statement of acceptance
- Premium amount and payment options
- Policy effective date
- Any conditions or requirements

### Referral Communications
- Specific information needed
- Reason for referral
- Expected timeline for decision
- Contact information for underwriter

### Decline Communications
- Clear reason for decline
- Specific guideline references
- Suggestions for improving eligibility
- Right to appeal information
