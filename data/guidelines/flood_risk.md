# Flood Risk Assessment Guidelines

## Overview
Flood risk assessment evaluates the potential for property damage from flooding events based on location, elevation, and historical data.

## FEMA Flood Zone Classifications

### High Risk Zones (A, V)
- **Zone A**: Areas with 1% annual chance of flooding
- **Zone V**: Coastal areas with 1% annual chance of flooding + wave action

### Moderate Risk Zones (B, X)
- **Zone B**: Areas with 0.2% annual chance of flooding
- **Zone X**: Areas between 100-year and 500-year floodplains

### Minimal Risk Zones (C, D)
- **Zone C**: Minimal flood hazard areas
- **Zone D**: Possible but undetermined flood hazards

## Underwriting Criteria

### Automatic Decline
- Properties in Zone V without elevation certificates
- Properties with >3 previous flood claims
- Properties below base flood elevation without mitigation

### Mandatory Referral
- Properties in Zone A requiring elevation verification
- Properties near water bodies with poor drainage
- Historical flooding in the area

### Acceptable Conditions
- Properties above base flood elevation
- Properties in Zones B, C, or X
- Proper flood mitigation measures in place

## Required Information
- FEMA flood zone determination
- Elevation certificate (if applicable)
- Flood history report
- Drainage assessment

## Premium Adjustments
- Zone V: +150% base premium
- Zone A: +100% base premium  
- Zone B/X: +25% base premium
- Zone C/D: No adjustment
