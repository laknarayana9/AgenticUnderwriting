# Construction Standards Guidelines

## Roof Requirements

### Acceptable Roof Types
**Excellent:**
- Metal roofing (Class A fire rating)
- Tile/Clay (Class A fire rating)
- Slate (Class A fire rating)

**Good:**
- Architectural asphalt shingles (Class A fire rating)
- Concrete tile (Class A fire rating)

**Fair:**
- 3-tab asphalt shingles (Class A fire rating)
- Wood shake (treated, Class B fire rating)

**Not Acceptable:**
- Untreated wood shake/shingles
- Roll roofing
- Damaged or deteriorated roofing materials

### Roof Age Requirements
- Metal/Tile/Slate: No age limit if well-maintained
- Architectural shingles: Max 20 years
- 3-tab shingles: Max 15 years
- Wood shake: Max 10 years (if treated)

## Foundation Requirements

### Acceptable Foundation Types
- Poured concrete foundation
- Concrete block foundation (reinforced)
- Post-tension slab
- Raised foundation (properly anchored)

### Foundation Issues Requiring Referral
- Visible cracks (>1/4 inch)
- Water damage or staining
- Uneven settling
- Poor drainage around foundation

## Electrical Systems

### Acceptable Systems
- Circuit breaker panels (post-1960)
- Updated wiring (copper, post-1970)
- Ground fault circuit interrupters (GFCI) in required areas
- Proper amperage for property size

### Systems Requiring Referral
- Fuse boxes
- Aluminum wiring (pre-1970)
- Knob and tube wiring
- Undersized electrical service

## Plumbing Systems

### Acceptable Materials
- Copper piping
- PEX piping
- CPVC (PVC)
- Modern galvanized steel (post-1960)

### Systems Requiring Referral
- Polybutylene piping (gray plastic)
- Lead pipes
- Galvanized steel (pre-1960)
- Visible leaks or corrosion

## HVAC Requirements

### Acceptable Systems
- Central air conditioning (less than 15 years old)
- Heat pumps (less than 15 years old)
- Modern furnaces (less than 20 years old)

### Systems Requiring Referral
- Window air conditioning units
- Wall heaters
- Systems older than 20 years
- Systems without proper maintenance
